WADE LEGACY

ABOUT

Former professional basketball player Dwayne Wade is regarded as one of the NBA's all-time great shooting guards. He was born in Chicago, Illinois, on January 17, 1982, and spent most of his childhood there until moving to the little town of Robbins. Wade improved his basketball skills while attending Harold L. Richards High School in Oak Lawn, Illinois. He then transferred to Marquette University in Milwaukee, Wisconsin, where he competed for the Marquette Golden Eagles in collegiate basketball. Wade was chosen by the Miami Heat in the first round of the NBA draught in 2003,
and he quickly established himself as a major member of the squad. He was voted the NBA Finals MVP and assisted the Heat in winning their first NBA title in 2006. With the Heat, Wade went on to win two additional NBA titles in 2012 and 2013. Wade was a 13-time NBA All-Star, an eight-time All-NBA selection, and a three-time NBA champion throughout his playing career.

In 2006, he was also voted the NBA Finals MVP. Furthermore, he captured a gold medal with the American men's basketball squad at the Beijing Summer Olympics in 2008. After the 2018–19 NBA season,
Wade retired from the league. He averaged 22.0 points, 5.4 assists, and 4.7 rebounds per game throughout the course of his career. He was renowned for his athleticism, versatility as a scorer, and clutch performances in crucial games.
He was later nicknamed “The Flash” by his former teammates for his ability to move quickly and dissapear infront of his defenders. Wade has participated in a variety of humanitarian and charitable activities away from the court. In 2003, he established the Wade's World Foundation, which offers assistance to neighbourhood groups that support kids in at-risk situations with their education, health, and social skills. He has worked to stop gun violence and support Gay rights and is an advocate for social justice causes. This site is meant to celebrate Wades legacy and informs fans on
how to be a passionate winner like “flash.”
